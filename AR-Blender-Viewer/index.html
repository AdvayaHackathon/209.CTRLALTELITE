Nalanda: Historical Legacy

Nalanda was founded in the 5th century CE as a renowned, international center of learning with a vast library and diverse curriculum. In the late 12th century, its destruction by Bakhtiyar Khalji—whose forces burned the library and buildings—marked a turning point that hastened the decline of Buddhism in India.

Rediscovered through 19th-century excavations, the ruins are now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and modern efforts (including the re-establishment of a new Nalanda University in 2010) aim to revive its enduring legacy.
